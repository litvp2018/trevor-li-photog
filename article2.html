summer Iceland trip 2018

In 2018, my family and I stayed in Iceland from the 2nd to 9th of August. During the week long trip we visited several attractions located within the city of Reykjavík as well as other destinations located further South towards the villages of Skógar and Vík. Here is a collection of some photos (both film and digital) that I have arranged in a roughly chronological order. Note that some of the photos below were included not because of their artistic value but rather for the sake of inclusion and completeness. Fun fact: I carried only a single lens for my DSLR for the entire trip: my Nikon Nikkor 35mm f/1.8G DX.

Halfway through the flight to Reykjavík and just before dawn, we ran into something that I was totally not expecting: Noctilucent clouds! They are a type of meterological phenomenon that is extremely rare and not very well understood. They're created when light is reflected off high altitude ice crystals (76-85 km in the mesosphere!) and are only visible during just before sunrise and after sunset. In order for them to be visible, sunlight must only illuminate the upper layers of the atmosphere while the the lower parts are still blocked by Earth's own shadow. They're also not a true type of cloud despite their name and appearance.
